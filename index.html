ARE CHARITABLE CHILDREN INSTITUTIONS INDISPENSABLE IN PROTECTION OF CHILDREN IN NEED OF CARE AND PROTECTION IN KENYA?

The position of Kenya with regard to children rights prior to 1989 was aligned with the common law perspective that children did not need any special protection since they were deemed to be sufficiently protected as part of the family. This position changed with the ratification of the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child in 1989 and the African Charter on the Rights and Welfare of the Child in 1990, both of which Kenya adopted without reservations in 1990 and 2000 respectively.

Pursuant to the provisions of Article 1 of the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child and Article 4 African Charter on the Rights and Welfare of the Child, Kenya enacted the Children Act No 8 of 2001 in order to give effect to the provisions of the two treaties. By extension the Act also intended to provide for the care and protection of children and the administration of children institutions.

Pursuant to the provisions of Article 1 of the United Nations Convention on the Rights of the Child and Article 4 African Charter on the Rights and Welfare of the Child, Kenya enacted the Children Act No 8 of 2001 in order to give effect to the provisions of the two treaties. By extension the Act also intended to provide for the care and protection of children and the administration of children institutions.

The Act provides that a child in need of care and protection includes a child who has been abandoned by their parents; one is found begging; who is prevented from receiving education; a female child likely to be subjected to harmful cultural practices; one who is exposed to domestic violence; one who is pregnant; one who has or is likely to be sexually abused and also one who is involved in the use or trafficking of harmful substances, among others.

In furtherance of this duty to protect children in need of care and protection, the 2001 Children Act provided for the establishment of Charitable Children Institutions. These were meant to be tools to assist the State safeguard the interests of children. They are defined under the Act as a home or institution, whether established a person, corporate, religious organization or Non-governmental Organization that has the approval of the council to provide care, protection, rehabilitation or control of children.

Since the commencement of the Children Act No. 18 of 2001, in 2002 there has been a proliferation of Charitable Children Institutions around the country to the extent that the government took notice. These Institutions are key in the protection of these vulnerable children
